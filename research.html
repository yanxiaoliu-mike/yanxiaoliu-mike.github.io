Publication

Rate Region of Scheduling a Wireless Network with Discrete Propagation Delays
with Professor Shenghao Yang and Jun Ma
Published in INFOCOM 2021.

We study the link scheduling problem of wireless networks where signal propagation delays are multiples of certain time interval. The problem can be modeled as a character of the independent sets of periodic graphs, which have infinitely many vertices. We show that the rate region of scheduling a network can be achieved using collision-free, periodic schedules, and derive a graphical approach to explicitly characterize the rate region. In particular, a collision-free schedule can be equivalent to a path in a graph called the scheduling graph induced by the network collision profile and the propagation delays, and hence the rate region is equal to the convex hull of the rate vectors associated with the cycles of the scheduling graph, which have bounded length. With the maximal independent set problem as a special case, calculating the whole rate region is NP hard and also hard to approximate. By exploring a partial order on the paths, we derive an algorithm to calculate a subset of the rate region more efficiently.Our results are also of independent interest for periodic graphs.Â

Weighted Parity-Check Codes for Channels with State and Asymmetric Channels
with Professor LI Cheuk Ting and Chih Wei Ling
will submit to ISIT 2021.

we introduce a new class of codes, called weighted parity-check codes, where each parity-check bit has a weight that indicates its likelihood to be one (instead of fixing each parity-check bit to be zero). It is applicable to a wide range of settings, e.g. asymmetric channels, channels with state and/or cost constraints, and the Wyner-Ziv problem, and can provably achieve the capacity. For the channel with state (Gelfand-Pinsker) setting, the proposed coding scheme has two advantages compared to the nested linear code. First, it achieves the capacity of any channel with state with binary input (e.g. asymmetric channels). Second, simulation results show that the proposed code achieves a smaller error rate compared to the nested linear code.

On the Bounds of Rate Regions by Scheduling Networks with Discrete Propagation Delay
with Professor Shenghao Yang and Yijun Fan
will submit to ISIT 2021.

We study wireless networks with large signal propagation delays, where the network model is time-slotted. We exploit the topology of a periodic graph and characterize the inner and outer bounds of rate regions by link scheduling, where it is known that exactly calculating the rate regions is NP hard and also hard to approximate. We discuss two examples based on our bounds. One is a line network of arbitrary length, whose rate region can be characterized by our tight bounds. Another is K-pair network of arbitrary size, where our bound is also tight on the rate vector achieving the maximum sum rate. We show our bounds recover the sum rate upper bound of single collision domain networks.

BATched Sparse code(BATS) Protocol
with Professor Shenghao Yang
Hong Kong government's "Intelligent Lamp" project.

BATched Sparse code(BATS) is proposed for communication through multi-hop networks with packet loss. Based on advanced network coding techniques, BATS Protocol is proposed to meet the on-going and up-coming data communication demands of various network applications, including IoT, underwater networks, sensor networks and vehicular networks. Such network communications are characterized by large-scale, multi-hop wireless networks, where the existing network communication protocols, like TCP/IP, cannot fulfill.
